Beignets

Description

Beignets (pronounced ben-yays) are the official state doughnut of Louisiana. These pillowy, deep-fried squares of yeasted dough are served piping hot and buried under a small mountain of powdered sugar. Unlike traditional doughnuts, they have no hole and are slightly more bread-like, making them the perfect companion to a cup of café au lait. Ingredients

  • The Dough :
    • 1 ½ cups warm water (110°F)
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
    • 1 large egg, beaten
    • 1 cup evaporated milk
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
    • 6 ½ to 7 cups all-purpose flour
  • The Frying & Finishing :
    • Vegetable or Peanut oil (for deep frying)
    • 2 cups powdered sugar (for dusting)

Cooking Directions

  1. Bloom the Yeast : In a large bowl, whisk together warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix the Wet Ingredients : Whisk in the beaten egg, evaporated milk, salt, and melted butter.
  3. Form the Dough : Gradually add 4 cups of flour, mixing until smooth. Switch to a dough hook (or use your hands) and add the remaining flour until a soft, slightly sticky dough forms.
  4. Rise : Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1–2 hours (or refrigerate overnight for better flavor).
  5. Cut : Roll the dough out on a floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness. Cut into 2-inch squares or rectangles.
  6. Fry : Heat oil in a deep pot to 360°F (182°C). Fry the dough in batches (3–4 at a time). They will puff up instantly. Flip them once they are golden brown (about 1 minute per side).
  7. Sugar : Drain briefly on paper towels, then immediately toss them into a bag or bowl filled with powdered sugar while still hot. Serve immediately.
